Subsystems

The team collaborates on different parts of the projects in 4 subsystems:
propulsions, avionics, structures, and recovery.

Propulsions

The propulsions subteam specializes in rocket engine design, analysis, manufacturing, and testing. The components of interest include:

  • The combustion chamber
  • Liquid oxidizer regulation & feed systems
  • Ground support equipment and systems
  • Predictive models of engine performance
  • Post-test analysis and iterative

    • Avionics

    Avionics is Responsible for building, programming, testing, and integrating the electronic control systems responsible for a variety of mission-critical functions:

  • Monitoring and controlling the rocket propulsion system
  • Recording of in-flight telemetry data
  • Broadcasting real-time heading and altitude data of the rocket to mission-control
  • Actuating pyrotechnic stage separation and recovery

    • Structures

    Responsible for designing and manufacturing the body of the rocket, including:

  • the nose cone
  • Body tubes
  • Fins and mounts
  • Interstage bulkheads
  • Electronics mounting & packaging
  • Propulsion systems mounting

    • Recovery

    Responsible for design, analysis, and testing of the recovery system which ensures that the vehicle and all data onboard safely returns to the ground. Key tasks involve:

  • Designing a dual deployment recovery system
  • Integrating the system into the vehicle
  • Calculating descent rate, parachute sizing, and pyrodex mass
  • Performing static ground testing of pyrotechnic separations under varying conditions
